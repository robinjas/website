Welcome to Springfield

Springfield, the capital city of Illinois, is rich in history and culture, famously known as the home of Abraham Lincoln, the 16th President of the United States, before he moved to the White House. Located in the central part of the state, Springfield is a hub of political activity, history, and educational resources, drawing visitors and residents alike to its numerous historical sites, museums, and attractions.

Adventure into the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum to immerse yourself in the life and legacy of Abraham Lincoln through interactive exhibits and rare artifacts. This museum not only chronicles Lincoln's journey from his early years in a log cabin to his transformative presidency but also provides insights into the Civil War era, offering a comprehensive view of the challenges he faced and the decisions that shaped the nation.

Lincoln Home National Historic Site

The Lincoln Home offers a glimpse into the life of Abraham Lincoln with guided tours of his beautiful family home, preserved in its 1860s condition.
