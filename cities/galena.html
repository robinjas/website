Welcome to Galena

Hidden in the northwest corner of Illinois, Galena is a living museum of 19th-century architecture, offering a view into a bygone era with its well-preserved buildings and charming streets. This picturesque town, once a booming lead mining community in the early 19th century, played a pivotal role in the economic development of Illinois. Its rich deposits of lead brought wealth and rapid growth, making it one of the most important settlements in the state during that period.

Galena is a destination that captivates visitors with its historical charm, scenic beauty, and a wide array of activities. Whether you're strolling through historic Main Street, paddling on the Galena River, or celebrating at a local festival, Galena offers a unique blend of experiences that resonate with history buffs, nature lovers, and adventure seekers alike.

Ulysses S. Grant Home

The Ulysses S. Grant Home is a key attraction in Galena, offering insight into the life of the 18th President of the United States and Civil War general, with the home preserved much as it was in the 1860s.
