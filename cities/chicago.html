Welcome to Chicago

Chicago, the third-largest city in the United States, stands out for its stunning architecture, vibrant arts scene, and diverse culinary experiences, making it a dynamic metropolis that captivates visitors and residents alike. The city's architectural prowess is showcased through its iconic skyline, dotted with engineering marvels such as the Willis Tower (formerly Sears Tower), the neo-Gothic Tribune Tower, and the sleek, aqua-blue contours of the Aqua Building. Architectural tours on foot and along the Chicago River offer insights into the city's innovative designs and the stories behind historic buildings that survived the Great Chicago Fire of 1871.

Chicago's allure extends into its iconic waterfront, the Navy Pier, which stretches into Lake Michigan and offers entertainment, shopping, dining, and cultural experiences. Navy Pier is home to the Chicago Children's Museum, a grand Ferris wheel, and the Chicago Shakespeare Theater, providing fun and education for all ages. The seasonal fireworks and boat tours available from the Pier offer stunning views of the city skyline and a unique perspective on Chicago's architectural wonders.

Wrigley Field

Wrigley Field is a historic baseball stadium located in the Lakeview neighborhood of Chicago, home to the Chicago Cubs. It is known for its ivy-covered outfield walls and classic manual scoreboard.
